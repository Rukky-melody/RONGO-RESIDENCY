RONGO FOUNDATION

About Us

Rongo Art Foundation is a youth-led cultural organisation based in Benin City, Nigeria, working at the intersection of heritage, artistic freedom and professional sustainability.

We exist because emerging artists in Nigeria often possess extraordinary talent but lack the structural support needed to thrive. Informal contracts, limited legal awareness, mobility barriers and digital exploitation risks continue to shape the creative landscape. We respond by building structure through training, advocacy, mentorship and strategic partnerships.

Our work includes the Benin Arts and Books Festival, which celebrates cultural expression and social dialogue, and the Rongo Artist Residency, a multidisciplinary programme that combines artistic production with legal literacy, AI protection awareness and mobility guidance.

We are committed to decentralising cultural infrastructure, strengthening professional standards and ensuring that artists are not merely creative, but protected and positioned for sustainable growth.

Rongo Art Foundation is rooted in heritage, driven by young leadership and building toward a more equitable creative future.

Our Mission

To strengthen artistic freedom, economic sustainability and international mobility for emerging and marginalized artists through capacity building, advocacy, research and strategic support systems.

Our Vision

We envision a creative ecosystem where artists are not only talented, but protected, economically empowered and institutionally supported.

Rooted in heritage. Focused on structure. Committed to the future.

Our Work

Cultural Preservation & Contemporary Dialogue

We revive and document Benin City’s artistic heritage while connecting it to contemporary practice. Our programming ensures that cultural memory is not static, but evolving.

Capacity Building & Professional Development

We equip artists with:

  • Legal literacy and contract awareness
  • Copyright and intellectual property education
  • AI and digital protection knowledge
  • Professional positioning & monetization

We help artists move from informal survival to structured practice.

Cross-Cultural Exchange

We foster collaboration between African creatives and global practitioners, encouraging dialogue, mobility and shared learning.

Advocacy & Policy Awareness

We promote the recognition of artists as professionals whose work requires protection, fair remuneration and institutional support.
